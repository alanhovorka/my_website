During the spring of 2015, myself and nine other students undertook the task of recording the oral histories of Ball State’s African American alumni. The last history book of Ball State University, published in 2001, contains few details about the experiences of students of color at one of Indiana’s most prominent institutions. We spent time researching the university’s history and the history of African Americans in the midwest and Indiana.

I interviewed Foster Stephens and Charles Greenwood. Greenwood was the first African American professional faculty member on campus.

The goal of this project was to produce primary research in the form of video interviews and transcripts that would be preserved in the university’s archives. Historians won’t be able to overlook African American history at Ball State the next time the institution’s history is written about because of this project.

The Ball State University African American Alumni Oral Histories collection provides online access to 20 interviews conducted with African Americans who attended or worked at Ball State University between 1950 and 2010 regarding their lives, careers, and college or graduate school experiences. Interviews were conducted and recorded by Ball State University Honors College and Public History Program students enrolled in the Oral History Workshop immersive learning course during the spring of 2015 under the direction of Dr. Michael Doyle, Director of Public History Program and Associate Professor in the Ball State University Department of History.
