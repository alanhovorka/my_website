Moon landing still impacting Muncie 45 years later

I began planning coverage of the 45th anniversary of the moon landing during the winter of 2014, knowing one of humanity's greatest achievement continues impacts Muncie and Ball State University. I went out with my theory and found three stories that support it.

Part One

The first part covers Muncie. On the outskirts of Muncie there sits the National Model Aviation Museum. Inside the museum hang hundreds of model aircraft, some as large as you or me. However, tucked away in a room of the museum sit model craft from straight from NASA's testing grounds. NASA engineers used these models to test designs for the space shuttle and other spacecraft. This story published July 7, 2014.

Part Two

The second part reflects how the space program touched the lives of people across the country. A Ball State faculty member's brother served as space shuttle pilot. I detail their relationship and how her brother became an astronaut. They grew up during the Apollo-era. He did a stint on the International Space Station. This story published July 14, 2014.
